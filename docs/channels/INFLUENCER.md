# Influencer Marketing

Influencer marketing involves partnering with individuals who have established audiences on social platforms to promote your products or services. The industry has matured significantly — pricing now reflects format complexity and engagement quality over follower counts, and compliance enforcement has intensified.

## Types of Influencer Collaboration

### 1. Sponsored Posts
- **Feed Posts:** Influencers share static or video content featuring your product.
- **Stories / Ephemeral Content:** 24-hour placements; lower cost, but no permanent record.

### 2. Short-Form Video
- **TikTok Videos / Instagram Reels:** High-reach vertical video. Most effective format for product discovery with younger demographics.
- **YouTube Shorts:** Growing inventory; lower rates than long-form integrations.

### 3. Long-Form Integrations
- **YouTube Dedicated Videos or Mid-Roll Mentions:** Higher trust and retention; significantly higher rates.

### 4. Product Reviews and Unboxing
- **Organic-feeling demos:** Showcase features, first impressions, or comparisons.

### 5. Giveaways and Contests
- **Engagement campaigns:** Influencers host contests to distribute your products, boosting followers and visibility.

### 6. Product Seeding
- Send free product to nano/micro influencers without a paid contract. Up to **83% of nano/micro influencers** are willing to create content for gifted product alone if the brand-product alignment is genuine — the most cost-efficient strategy available to small businesses.

## 2026 Rate Benchmarks

Pricing has moved away from follower counts toward format complexity, engagement rate, and audience geography.

| Tier | Followers | Instagram Post | Instagram Story / Reel | TikTok Video | YouTube Integration |

| **Nano** | 1,000–10,000 | $20–$200 | $25–$300 / Story; $100–$500 / Reel | $20–$500 | $100–$500 (video); $50–$120 (Shorts) |

## FTC Disclosure Requirements (2026)

Enforcement has escalated significantly. Civil penalties now reach **$53,088 per violation**.

### What's required

- **Above the fold:** Disclosures must appear before any "read more" click or swipe.
- **Unambiguous language:** `#ad` or `Sponsored by [Brand]` — explicitly. Vague tags like `#partner`, `#collab`, or `#ambassador` do **not** satisfy the standard.
- **Video dual disclosure:** Any video integration on YouTube, TikTok, or Instagram Reels requires:
  1. A **verbal callout** within the first 30 seconds.
  2. A **prominent on-screen text overlay** visible during the sponsored segment.
- **Platform labels are insufficient:** Instagram's "Paid Partnership" tag and TikTok's branded content toggle do not satisfy FTC requirements on their own. Creators must include their own written and verbal disclosure.

### Written disclosure policy
Small businesses running influencer campaigns must maintain a written disclosure policy and provide it to every creator prior to campaign launch. This creates a documented compliance record.

## Benefits

- **Trust and credibility:** Leverages influencers' existing audience relationships.
- **Extended reach:** Access new, engaged demographics organically.
- **Authentic content:** Creator-generated content outperforms brand-produced ads on native platforms.
- **Cost efficiency:** Nano/micro tiers and product seeding offer strong ROI relative to traditional paid media.

## Setting Up Influencer Marketing

1. **Define objective:** Product awareness, conversion, user-generated content (UGC), or community building.
2. **Identify creators:** Use a platform like Elev8or or Collabstr; filter by engagement rate (not follower count), audience location, and niche.
3. **Vet for authenticity:** Run fake follower checks; review past sponsored post performance.
4. **Set terms in writing:** Deliverables, timeline, usage rights (especially for repurposing as paid ads), and disclosure requirements.
5. **Brief but don't script:** Provide talking points, key claims, and brand guidelines — over-scripting kills authenticity.
6. **Track performance:** Use unique UTM links or discount codes; request creator analytics screenshots post-campaign.
